## Question: What is the Internal Locus of Control? What is the key point in the video?

### Internal Locus of Control (definition)  
An internal locus of control is the belief that your own actions — your effort, decisions and behavior — shape the outcomes in your life, rather than luck or external circumstances.

### Key Point from the Video  
The video argues that having an internal locus of control (believing results come from your effort rather than outside factors) helps you stay motivated and take consistent action, even when faced with difficulties.

### Key Points to Build a Growth Mindset

- See challenges as opportunities.
- Use the power of “yet” (I can’t do it **yet**).
- Learn from failures and setbacks.
- Believe abilities can grow with effort.
- Focus on learning, not proving.
- Replace negative self-talk with growth statements.
- Seek feedback and use it for improvement.
- Stay consistent and persistent.
- Celebrate effort, strategy, and progress.
- Be open to trying new things.

## Question: What are your ideas to take action and build Growth Mindset?

### Actionable Ideas to Build a Growth Mindset (as a Software Developer)

- Take challenges as learning tasks instead of avoiding them.  
- Break big goals into small, consistent daily practice steps.  
- Learn from errors by reviewing what went wrong and correcting it.  
- Ask for feedback from peers/seniors and actually apply it.  
- Replace “I can’t do this” with “I can learn this.”  
- Try new tools, concepts, and problems regularly.  
- Track progress weekly to see improvement over time.  
- Focus on effort, patience, and persistence instead of perfection.  
- Celebrate small wins in learning new skills.  
- Stay curious and keep experimenting with code.
